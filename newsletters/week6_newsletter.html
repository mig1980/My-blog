Portfolio dips as tech, gold, and cyclicals retreat broadly
The GenAi Chosen portfolio declined -5.82% this week, falling below its starting value for the first time since inception and bringing total returns to -3.01%. A broad risk-off shift swept through markets, pressuring high-beta tech, cyclicals, and even traditional safe-haven plays. Nearly every stock in the portfolio sold off as investor appetite faded and volatility picked up. This sets the stage for a deeper look at the sectors and names most affected by the downturn.
Key Insights
Tech Leads Selloff
Western Digital, Seagate, and Palantir posted sharp losses as high-beta AI and data infrastructure names fell out of favor. The move reflects heightened macro uncertainty and a broad pullback from growth exposures. These declines weighed heavily on weekly portfolio performance.
Gold Miners Slip
Newmont dropped despite gold's typical defensive profile, signaling that miners were not spared in the risk-off rotation. The decline erased some of the portfolio's prior resilience from precious metals exposure. This shows how correlations can tighten during broad market stress.
Cyclicals Lose Momentum
Royal Caribbean and NRG Energy both retreated as cyclical sectors struggled to hold recent gains. Travel and energy names, which had benefitted from earlier momentum, were unable to withstand the market's defensive pivot. Their pullbacks contributed to the portfolio's broad-based weekly weakness.
